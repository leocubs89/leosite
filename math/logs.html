What is a logarithm?

An logarithm or "log" could be described as the inverse function of an expoanants

suppose we wanted to find the expoanant in 2^x = 8. to solve for x we can use logs to bring it down from the expoanant.

since we have a base of 2, we multiply both sides by log_2 like here: log_2(2^x) = log_2(8) to get x = log_2(8)

now to solve log_2(8) we can use our calculator, or if we want to do it by hand we count how many times we have to multiply 1 by 2 to get 8

1 ∙ 2 ∙ 2∙ 2 = 8, we multiplied by 2 three times, giving us an answer of 3

basic log rules

The 4 basic laws of logs are as follows

  1. log_b(1) = 0
  2. log_b(b) = 1
  3. log_b(b^k) = k
  4. b^log_b(k) = k

now lets take a closer look at these laws

zero rule: log_b(1) = 0

very simple rule stemming from the exponant law that x^0 = 1

identity rule: log_b(b) = 1

This rule is a simplified version of the next rule

This could also be written log_b(b^1) = 1

we can clearly see the goal of a log is to bring down an exponant here

log of an exponant rule: log_b(b^k) = k

the mathmatical function of a log is in full display here

a log is the opposite function of an expoanant in the sense that when taking a log of an exponant and both have the same base then the answer is the exponant itself.
