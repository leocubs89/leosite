Connection Types

There are two types of connections between componants

  1. Series
  2. Parallel

When connected in series the end of one componant is connected to the front of the other

In parallel connection the fronts and the backs of each componant is connected

Ohms law

the three setups for ohms law

  1. V = IR
  2. R = V/I
  3. I = V/R

This is the most basic and fundimental equation of electrical engineering.

Lets look at our first practice problem

schematic

First question

Here is a 10V Voltage source connected to a 5 Ohm Resistor

If we know V=10 and R=5 then what is I?

Solution

We see above I = V/R

plugging this is we get

I = 10/5 = 2 Amps

Adding Resistors Series

Now we have two 5 Ohm resistors connected in series

When adding resistors in series the equation is simply

R1 + R2 = Rt, Rt = total resistance

Solution

plugging into R1 + R2 = Rt

5 + 5 = 10 Ohms

Lets find current

I = 10/10 = 1 Amp

Adding Resistors Parallel

Now we have two 5 Ohm resistors connected in parallel

When adding resistors in parallel we use the equation

(1/R1 + 1/R2)^-1 = Rt, Rt = total resistance

Solution

plugging into (1/R1 + 1/R2)^-1 = Rt

(1/5 + 1/5)^-1 =

(2/5)^-1 = 5/2 = 2.5

Lets find current

I = 10/2.5 = 4 Amps

Voltage divider

a voltage divider is two resistors connected in series.

we are going to be looking for the voltage across R2

to find the voltage across a resistor we must first know the current going through it

but first to find current we need to find Rt

Once we have the current we can use Ohms law V = IR2

Solution

plugging into R1 + R2 = Rt

2 + 3 = 5 Ohms

I = 10/5 = 2 Amps

finding V2

V2 = (2)(2) = 4 Volts
